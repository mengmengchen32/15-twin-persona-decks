Better Answers Require a Fuller Picture

When a consumer stops buying a product at one retailer, the interesting question is: did they switch brands, switch channels, or stop buying altogether? That answer only exists when you can see across retailers.

The more complete the view of a consumer's purchasing life, the more precisely Twin Personas can model what happens next. That's what unlocks the questions CPG brands care about most:

Competitive Switching
Grounded in purchase timing data

When is Ryan most likely to switch to Kroger?

Days 12–16 of his coffee cycle, and right before a camping trip. His K-Cup orders follow a 13.8-day cadence. The 2–3 day window before he reorders on Amazon is when a Kroger Ship-to-Home offer competes directly with Subscribe & Save. Separately, his MSR 6-person tent purchase signals group meal planning — a moment Kroger can intercept with charcoal, bulk meat, and snack bundles.
Evidence: 64 orders / 30 months = 13.8-day avg cycle; MSR tent + travel gear purchases
